How FableWall Works

1

You Submit a Claim

Paste any news headline or statement you have seen online or received on social media.

2

Machine Learning Analysis

The system uses TF-IDF similarity matching and a Naive Bayes classifier trained on over 72,000 news articles to analyse the claim.

3

Fact-Check Database Search

FableWall searches Google's Fact Check Tools database, which indexes thousands of verified fact-checks from credible organisations worldwide.

4

You See Real Evidence

Results show actual verdicts from human fact-checkers with source links you can read yourself, not just an AI opinion.

Why FableWall?

Evidence first

FableWall prioritises actual fact-check records and source links so decisions are based on verification, not guesswork.

Local claim memory

Claims seeded from the local database help the app improve over time with relevant examples tailored to your context.

Fast results

The platform returns a verdict quickly, with a clear summary and related news coverage that makes sense at a glance.

About the creator

Taofeek Olamilekan is the founder behind FableWall

Taofeek is a final year Information Technology student at the University of Ilorin. He built FableWall with the aim of curbing the rampant spread of misinformation, making verification more transparent and easier for students, journalists, curious readers, and everyday non-technical users.

FableWall combines Python, Flask, local machine learning, and verified fact-check evidence so users can check claims with clear reasoning, not just another internet opinion.

What this project stands for

  • Evidence first: fact-checks before speculation
  • Clarity first: simple results for complex claims
  • Education first: built for learners, researchers, and the curious
